PSYSENSE INTERVIEW ANSWER EVALUATION RUBRIC

DIMENSION 1 — CLARITY
Definition:
How clearly the candidate communicates ideas.

Scoring:
0–2 → Very confusing, broken or hard to understand.
3–5 → Partially understandable but lacks clarity.
6–8 → Mostly clear and understandable.
9–10 → Extremely clear, precise and well articulated.


DIMENSION 2 — RELEVANCE
Definition:
How well the answer addresses the interview question.

Scoring:
0–2 → Completely irrelevant.
3–5 → Some relevance but goes off topic.
6–8 → Mostly relevant.
9–10 → Fully relevant and focused.


DIMENSION 3 — STRUCTURE
Definition:
Logical organisation of answer (STAR or storytelling flow).

Look for:
Situation → Task → Action → Result

Scoring:
0–2 → No structure, random thoughts.
3–5 → Some order but weak flow.
6–8 → Good logical flow.
9–10 → Excellent structured storytelling.


DIMENSION 4 — DEPTH
Definition:
Level of insight, detail and reflection.

Scoring:
0–2 → Very shallow answer.
3–5 → Basic explanation only.
6–8 → Good detail and reflection.
9–10 → Deep thoughtful explanation with learning or impact.


DIMENSION 5 — CONFIDENCE
Definition:
Tone of ownership and confidence in communication.

Scoring:
0–2 → Hesitant or unsure tone.
3–5 → Moderate confidence.
6–8 → Confident expression.
9–10 → Strong leadership confidence.


DIMENSION 6 — PROBLEM SOLVING
Definition:
Explanation of reasoning and decision making approach.

Scoring:
0–2 → No reasoning shown.
3–5 → Minimal reasoning.
6–8 → Logical reasoning visible.
9–10 → Strong analytical thinking and justification.


Evaluator must also generate:
• Short behavioral reasoning summary
• Mention strengths
• Mention improvement areas
