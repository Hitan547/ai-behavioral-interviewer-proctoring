# PsySense AI - Candidate Consent Notice

Last updated: May 5, 2026

## Purpose

This notice explains what candidates should understand before taking an interview on PsySense AI.

This is a pilot-ready draft and should be reviewed by legal counsel before external public use.

## Candidate Consent Statement

Before starting the interview, candidates should be shown a consent notice similar to:

```text
By continuing, I understand that this interview may be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed using AI-assisted tools. PsySense may evaluate my answers, generate structured scores, and collect interview integrity signals such as tab switching, fullscreen exits, copy/paste attempts, multiple-face detection, and engagement indicators.

I understand that AI-generated scores and proctoring signals are decision-support information for recruiter review. Final hiring decisions are made by human reviewers, not by PsySense alone.

I confirm that I have read this notice and agree to continue with the interview.
```

## What Candidates Should Know

Candidates should be informed that:

- Their interview answers may be recorded or processed.
- Audio may be transcribed into text.
- AI may generate answer scores, feedback, and summaries.
- Recruiters may review transcripts, scores, reports, and proctoring signals.
- The system may collect browser and camera-related integrity signals.
- AI outputs are not final hiring decisions.
- A human recruiter or hiring team remains responsible for final decisions.

## Proctoring Signals Collected

Depending on deployment settings, PsySense may collect:

- Browser tab switch events.
- Window focus changes.
- Fullscreen exits.
- Copy or paste attempts.
- DevTools attempt indicators.
- Multiple-face detection.
- Engagement or attention signals.

These signals are intended to help recruiters understand interview context. They are not automatic proof of misconduct.

## Candidate Environment Expectations

Candidates should be asked to:

- Use a stable internet connection.
- Use a working microphone and camera if required.
- Sit in a quiet and well-lit environment.
- Avoid switching tabs or leaving fullscreen unless necessary.
- Avoid using external help unless explicitly allowed.
- Contact the recruiter if technical issues occur.

## Accessibility and Fairness

Candidates should have a way to notify the recruiter if they need accommodations or if the interview environment creates unfair conditions.

Examples:

- Disability-related accommodations.
- Camera/microphone limitations.
- Poor network conditions.
- Shared living or work environment.
- Browser/device limitations.

Recruiters should consider these factors when reviewing scores and proctoring signals.

## Candidate Questions or Data Requests

Candidates should be given a contact channel for:

- Questions about the interview process.
- Requests for accommodation.
- Data access or deletion requests.
- Concerns about AI evaluation or proctoring signals.

## Recommended In-App Checkbox

Before the interview starts, the app should require a checkbox:

```text
I understand and agree to the AI-assisted interview, transcription, scoring, and integrity signal collection described above.
```

The interview should not begin until the candidate confirms consent.
